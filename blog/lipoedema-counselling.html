More Than the Physical:
Emotional Support for Lipoedema

Lipoedema is usually described in physical terms. The pain, the swelling, the way the body changes and resists the things that are meant to help. What gets far less attention is everything else it carries.

Many women find out about lipoedema after years of not being believed. Of being told to eat less and move more. Of being measured against a body that was never going to respond the way they were promised it would. By the time there is finally a name for it, there is often a great deal of frustration, grief and self-doubt sitting alongside the diagnosis.

This is a condition I understand closely, and one I care about deeply. I know how isolating it can feel, especially with something so often misunderstood, or barely known about at all.

Living with lipoedema can mean grieving a body you expected to have. It can mean carrying shame that was never yours to carry, managing daily pain and holding the slow and often lonely work of compression, movement and self-care. It can mean real fear about what the years ahead will look like. It can shape how you feel about being seen, about clothes, about intimacy and about taking up space.

As a psychotherapeutic counsellor I am not here to treat the condition itself. What I offer is a space for the part that so often goes unsupported. The feelings, the history and the toll of it.

In our work together we might explore the impact of a late or missed diagnosis, make sense of difficult experiences within healthcare and support, or find a way of relating to your body that feels less like a fight. We might sit with the anger, the tiredness or the loss, and give those feelings somewhere to go. There may be big decisions ahead too, about conservative management or surgery, and whatever you choose, the feelings that come with it are something we can think through together.

None of this has to be the whole story. With the right support the fear can soften and the shame can loosen its hold. It becomes possible to feel more at home in your body and your life again, with ease where there was struggle, a steadier relationship with yourself and more room for the parts of life that lipoedema had pushed to the edges. With time and support, many women find their way back to a life that feels like their own again.

There is no pressure to arrive with the right words or to have made sense of any of it first. We begin wherever you are.

If you are living with lipoedema and you are tired of carrying the emotional part of it on your own, you are welcome here.
